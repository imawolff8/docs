Categories help you keep your bookmarks organized and easy to find. Instead of scrolling through a flat list of every link you have saved, you can group related bookmarks together — by project, by tool type, by environment, or however best fits your workflow.

## Creating a Category

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in as admin">
    Navigate to `http://YOUR_SITE/index.php?c=login` and sign in with your admin credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Admin Panel">
    Click **Admin Panel** in the bottom toolbar to enter the administration interface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Category Management">
    Inside the Admin Panel, locate and click **Category Management** to see your existing categories and the controls for managing them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Category">
    Click the **Add Category** button to open the category creation form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the category details">
    Type a name for the category. If you want it to be a sub-category, choose a **Parent Category** from the dropdown. Leave the parent field empty to create a top-level category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The new category immediately appears in the category sidebar on the main dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sub-Categories

NextNav supports two levels of organization: top-level categories and one level of sub-categories nested beneath them. To create a sub-category, select an existing top-level category as the **Parent Category** when filling in the creation form.

Sub-categories appear indented under their parent in the category sidebar, making it easy to drill down into a specific group without losing sight of the broader structure.

## Editing and Deleting Categories

To rename or delete a category, return to **Admin Panel** → **Category Management**, find the category in the list, and use the edit or delete controls next to it.

<Note>
  Deleting a category does **not** delete the bookmarks inside it. Any bookmarks that belonged to the deleted category become uncategorized and remain accessible from the main dashboard view.
</Note>

<Note>
  You can have unlimited categories, but a clean structure with 5–10 top-level categories is easier to navigate day-to-day. Consider grouping less-used links under broader categories rather than creating a new category for every service.
</Note>

## Assigning Bookmarks to Categories

When adding or editing a bookmark, select the target category from the **Category** dropdown in the bookmark form. You can reassign a bookmark to a different category at any time by editing it and choosing a new category.

<Tip>
  Use the category sidebar on the main dashboard to filter bookmarks by category with a single click — no searching required when you know exactly which group a link belongs to.
</Tip>
