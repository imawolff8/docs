NextNav's search bar sits at the top of the dashboard and is always within reach. Whether you have ten bookmarks or a thousand, search lets you skip category browsing entirely and jump straight to any link in seconds.

## Using Search

Type a keyword into the search box at the top of the page. NextNav performs fuzzy matching against bookmark names and URLs in real time — results update as you type, with no need to press Enter. The bookmark grid narrows to show only the matches, and clicking any result opens the link directly.

To exit search and return to the full bookmark grid, press **Escape** or clear the search box.

## Search Tips

Getting the most out of NextNav's search is straightforward once you understand how it works:

- **Searches across all categories simultaneously** — you do not need to be browsing a specific category before searching. Every bookmark in your NextNav instance is in scope.
- **Partial words match** — typing a fragment is enough. For example, searching for `git` will surface bookmarks named "GitHub", "GitLab", "Gitea", and any URL containing `git`.
- **Click a result to open it** — results are live links. Clicking a bookmark card in the filtered grid opens the link just as it would from the full grid.
- **Clear to reset** — pressing **Escape** or deleting the text in the search box instantly restores the full grid view.

<Tip>
  Giving bookmarks descriptive names — rather than leaving them as auto-filled domain names — makes them much easier to find later. A name like "Home Lab — Portainer" is far more searchable than "192.168.1.50".
</Tip>

## AI Search (If Enabled)

NextNav can optionally integrate AI-powered search to match natural language descriptions to your bookmarks. Instead of needing to remember an exact name or URL fragment, you can describe what you are looking for in plain language and NextNav will surface the most relevant results.

If you do not see an AI search option in your dashboard, this feature may not be enabled on your instance. Contact your NextNav admin to find out whether AI search can be activated for your deployment.
