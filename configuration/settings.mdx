NextNav's Settings panel is where you control global behavior for your instance — from how bookmarks are displayed to whether guests can browse your links without logging in. Every change you make here applies site-wide and takes effect the moment you save.

## Opening Settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your NextNav instance">
    Navigate to your NextNav URL and sign in with your admin credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Admin Panel">
    Click the **Admin Panel** button in the bottom toolbar of the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Settings">
    Select **Settings** from the Admin Panel navigation menu to open the global configuration page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Settings

Use the options below to tailor your NextNav instance to your preferences. Each setting is saved independently — click **Save** after making changes.

<Accordion title="Site Title">
  The name displayed in the browser tab and the page header of your dashboard. Set this to something meaningful, like your name or your organization's name, so the tab is easy to identify across browser windows.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Homepage Layout">
  Choose between **Grid** and **List** view for your bookmark dashboard. Grid view displays bookmarks as visual tiles, while List view presents them in a compact, scannable format. Switch between them any time without affecting your bookmark data.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Link Open Behavior">
  Control whether clicking a bookmark opens it in a **new tab** or the **same tab**. New tab is the default and is recommended so your NextNav dashboard stays open while you browse.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Guest Access">
  Toggle whether unauthenticated visitors can view bookmarks marked as public. When **enabled**, anyone who visits your NextNav URL can see public bookmarks without logging in. When **disabled**, the login screen is shown to all unauthenticated users. See [User Management](/configuration/user-management) for more on public vs. private bookmarks.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  Changes take effect immediately after saving — no server restart required. Your dashboard reflects updates the next time the page loads.
</Note>

## Resetting to Defaults

NextNav does not include a one-click "reset all settings" button in the UI. To restore default values, log in as admin, return to **Admin Panel → Settings**, and manually revert each field to its original value.

If your settings are misconfigured and you can no longer access the UI, you can reset them directly by editing the SQLite database. Connect to your server, open `data/db.sqlite` with a SQLite client, and modify the `settings` table. For Docker deployments, use `docker exec -it nextnav /bin/sh` to get a shell inside the container before accessing the file.
